Virat Kohli

THE RUN MACHINE

Virat Kohli (born 5 November 1988) is an Indian international cricketer and former captain of the India national cricket team. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsman of all time and the best of his time.[4] Kohli plays as a right-handed batsman for Royal Challengers Bangalore in the Indian Premier League and for Delhi in Indian domestic cricket. With 40 wins out of 68 tests, Kohli is India's most successful test captain,and one of the most successful test captains of all time. He holds the record for being the highest run scorer in both IPL and in T20 internationals.He is also the highest run scorer in T20 WC history.He was chosen as the player of the decade by ICC in 2020. Kohli made his Test debut in 2011.[5] He reached the number one spot in the ICC rankings for ODI batsmen for the first time in 2013.[6] He has won Man of the Tournament twice at the ICC World Twenty20 (in 2014 and 2016). He is also the leading run scorer of all time in T20 Internationals as well as T20 World Cups. Kohli has been the recipient of multiple awards– most notably the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy (ICC Men's Cricketer of the Decade): 2011–2020; Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy (ICC Cricketer of the Year) in 2017 and 2018; ICC Test Player of the Year (2018); ICC ODI Player of the Year (2012, 2017, 2018) and Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World (2016, 2017 and 2018).[7] At the national level, he was awarded the Arjuna Award in 2013, the Padma Shri under the sports category in 2017[8] and the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award, the highest sporting honour in India, in 2018.
